Korean Winter Birds Monitoring Project




What is Winter Wings?

Winter Wings is a Citizen Science project conducted by researchers of Ewha Womans Univertsity and the National Museum of Natural History of Paris. This project aims to set up a winter long term monitoring of birds in Korea. Observations can be done as many times as you want, where you want, as long as you follow our protocol! The data collected will help scientists to better understand birds populations composition, distribution and abondance in Korea. This program is supported by companies, organisation and a networks of birders such as Donga Science, Birds Korea and Birding Korea. Join us!

Anyone can participate!

The data are collected by volonteers of all ages and skills levels following the protocol. We provide you some documentation so that you can learn about the most common birds and how to identify them.
The more data we have, the better it is for our analysis!

Protocol

Why should I follow the protocol?

A scientific protocol is a reproductible method which allow scientists to compare the observations between each other and answer to some research questions.

How does it work?

1

Choose an observation site and note the GPS coordinates

If you want, you can install a feeder. In this case, the feeder and its surroundings will be your observation site

2

Observe the birds according to the protocol

You need to observe birds around you during 2 sessions of 5 minutes. You count the maximum of individuals of the same species seen in the same time

3

Send us your data

Send us your data by filling the survey form

4

... and do it again!

Do it whenever, wherever, as many times as you want! The more the better for our analysis!

How to watch?

A five minute observation is all you need!

  • count 5 minute observation as a session. (you can optionally do 2 additional 10 minutes sessions)
  • Take into account all the birds than you can see around you: birds in the feeders, bushes and trees, on the ground, or flying in the area
  • Make a tally and count the maximum of individuals in view
  • If there are no birds during the period when you count, write “No birds”
